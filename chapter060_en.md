# Chapter 60: Royal Meeting

Severus looked around himself as he appeared in Buckingham Palace.
Besides him his best friend, Lucius Malfoy, smiled to him.

Although he was used to the luxury of Malfoy manor, and in some capacity of the ancestral Prince residence, the Potios Master had to strain to hold back a surprised whistle at the sight of the imposing stairs covered in red in front of him.

Delicate panelling and marquetry, masterful Muggle paintings, gold leaf gilding…
He was undeniably in the royal palace.

The two wizards automatically stepped away from the hearth of the chimney htat had made them arrive so that the others could leave easily.
Thus, the members of the Five Families appeared one after the other in the home of the Queen of the United Kindgom.
Nearly immediately, the unconsciously formed a half-circle behind Severus Prince and Lucius Malfoy, looking at the place with curiosity.

Despite himself, the heir of King Artus gulped and prevented himself from adjusting his collar before looking at his friends and allies.
He was uneasy, as he had until recently merely been Severus Snape, former Death Eather, Half-Blood, and despised professor in Hogwarts.
Nothing had really changed in this apart from his surname and the prestige it brought…
And what prestige!

Severus noted everyone's expressions…
The members of the Five Families were all standing in front of a lit up chimney that Muggles could not see.
Vladmir and Katya were giving Kevin comforting smiles, as he was uneasy in this royal pomp.
Damocles Rowle was looking at him with a mischeviousness that reminded Severus of the Weasley twins.
Amelia Bones was doing her best reassuring her niece, while Theodore Nott was squeezing his son's shoulder in a sign of support.

Severus, as straight and stiff as usual, looked at them with a gaze that could seem severe if you did not know him, the one that had traumatised generations of Hogwarts students.
As all the other members of the Five Families behind him, he was wearing a particular outfit that made him more intimidating than he was wearing his teaching robes.
A cermonial outfit that the Five Families had not shown in ceturies, made from the most expensive fabrics in the magical world and that differed slightly between men and women.
Theodore Nott Sr. was the one that it fit the best, according to the Potions Master.

The cape was the same for everyone, falling to the ground, deep black, made from the leather of dragons that had gone extinct long ago, lined with volcano tiger fur.
The cape's tie, in solid gold, had large braids wearing the coats-of-arms of the different Houses.
Below, men and women from the Five Families were wearing robes made from a deep purple brocarde embroidered with gold, the colours of the Five Families.
The motifs intertwined both the coats-of-arms and protection runes.
And one should not forget the ceremonial swords, as richly decorated as the rest; it took all the outfits in at the waist, on the opposite side from their wand holsters.
King Artus's wizards wielded both bladed weapons and magic, and this sword showed the fact that had long been forgotten in most of the magical world.

Amelia, Susan, and Katya wore an outfit made form Acromentula silk underneath those robes
