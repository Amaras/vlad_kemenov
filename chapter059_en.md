# Chapter 59: The Second Task

It was not hard to know where Vladmir Kemenov was in Hogwarts.
All the students whispered rumours saying one of the Ravenclaws had gon crazy, mulbing curses aplenty and giving off strong Dark Magic waves through the school.
According to the students, one only needed to follow the curses and dark fog in the corridor to find the Russian wizard.

"Vladmir," Kevin had tried to calm him down for a little bit, getting hit back by a wall of rage.

"WHAT?" the Russian finally roared, stopping his pacing in the middle of Gryffindor tower.

"You are scaring the first years," Neville said calmly, pointing to a corner of his common room where three young children were huddling.
He looked at his friend for a few seconds, before continuing to read his treaty on subaquatic mediterranean plants.

"Don't give a shit," Vlad growled.
"He knew, that son of a b…"

"Vladmir Vassilievitch!" Hermione warned, snapping her book shut.

"He is a primetime bastard!" Vladmit spat out.
"He knew perfectly well that the second task is tomorrow.
Then why is he putting his fucking meeting the same evening?
He is a son of a bitch!"

A small knock could be heard on the entrance door of the common room.
Neville looked at his friend ruminate his anger again for a few seconds, before standing up, sighing.

"Cedric…"

"I'm looking for Vlad," the Hufflepuff sighed.

"He is not very motivated to think about your strategy for tomorro," Neville sighed, stepping away from the entrance.

The Hufflepuff entered the Gryffindor common room and observed his teammate for a few seconds.
He perfectly remembered the furious light in his gaze when he had heard that Lucius had organised a meeting between the Five Families and the Muggle Queen of the United Kingdom the day of the Second Task of the Tri-Wizard Tournament.
Not the day before or after, no… the same day.

It happened on the day they had caught Narcissa formerly Black or Malfoy.
Cedric would remember this day all his life, he knew it.
It was the day that he had really stepped foot in the illegal part of the magical world.

Apparently, it had been an Initiation for Kevin, Fred, George, and himself, the last trial to becoe a true member of the Mirage Court.
Now, he bore on his left arm the same tattoo as Neville and Severus, the _Undeground_'s symbol.

Cedric briefly remembered Neville guiding them somewhere in London, during a Hogsmeade day out.
He had guided them to an underground place to be introduced to three masked people…
The Triads.
He had never been this afraid in his life.

That day had been strange.
For once, Vladmir had not been present, preferring a swim in the Black Lake with his Bulgarian and Russian friends.
He had understood afterwards that Vladmir belonged to the Russian Court, the _Semirov_.
He had no reason to be there the day of his introduction into the British Court…

Cedric felt a shiver despite his best effort and distractedly smiled at the young Ravenclaw next to him.
He had lied, smuggled, and tortured a woman…
He had been regularly sick from that, and he had seen that the twins were also affected.
Before realising that the school Matron, Madam Pomfrey, had a Mediwitch background and was bound to the medical secret.
Vladmir had forced the three oldest Alliance members to see her and tell her the reasons of their problems…

> "So, Mister Kemenov did create a cell in Hogwarts," the Mediwitch sighed wearily.
>
> "How did you know?" Fred had asked, hesitating a little.
>
> "The Mirage Court's tattoos are not always camouflaged…" Poppy Pomfrey had sighed again.

"Did you find a way to survive underwater for an hour?" Neville worried.

"Yes, but I have to talk about it with Vladmir," Cedric sighed, sitting down on a settee to look at his still furious friend.

"How?" Hemione asked distractedly, reading her book again.
She was, however, still looking over Vladmir from the corner of her eye to correct him verbally or intervene if he lost control of his magic.

"I have found a partial metamorphosis, except…"

"Except Vladmir is catastrophically bad at Transfiguration," Kevin mumbled.
"And he will refuse to use a spell."

"Apparently, his parents were Transfiguration experts," Neville chuckled.
"Mum has not stopped lauding Lily's talents, who allegedly transformed the Marauders into a bunch of gnomes after one of their pranks."

"I did not know your father knew the Marauders…"

"They were all in Gryffindor," Neville sighed.
"But in that case, he got in the middle without wanting it…
Lily could be mean when she was furious, according to Dad…"

"Which makes me thing of someone," Kevin mumbled, looking at his best friend now stopped in front of the fire, looking daggers at him like it could prevent the second task, or better yet, the royal meeting.

"Fuck off Kevin," Vlad said slowly, turning to his friends.

"Love you too, dear," Kevin chuckled.
"You've got work for tomorrow, so stop moaning and go work on the second task!"

Ceric could not stop himself from shivering at the look of pure hatred the Russian gave the Ravenclaw.
It only made the latter smile wider, to Cedric astonishment.

"You are crazy, you know that?" Hemione said calmly, turning a page of her book.
"Vlad, go with Cedric.
You Must not finish this task in second place, it is a case of honour for the Alliance.
Kevin, we still have work on those bloody enchantments."

"Don't you think it would be time to call for Professor Lupin's help?" Kevin moaned.

The two most gifted students in Hogwarts had spent the greatest part of a week hitting a brick wall on the enchantments on the Marauder's Map.
While they had managed to create identical ones, they had not managed to get a list of people present in the castle at any given time.
Remus Lupin, creator of the map, would be able to help them.

Hermione snapped her book shut and gave her friends a piercing stare.

"We will try until the holidays come, then we'll call ofr help.
What are you two still doing here?"

Vladmir rolled his eyes, ignoring Gryffindor's amused look as he observed the scene from his portrait.

"Come Ced before we make her mad.
She can be quite scary when she's like that."

"You're the one to say it?" the Gryffindor took offence.
"Come back her Vladmir Vassilievitch Kemenov"

Cedric could only laugh when Vladmir smartly chose to flee, any fury gone with a mocking sneer.

"So, any idea for the second task?" Vladmir asked eventually, keeping his hands in his pockets.

"Bubble-Head Charm, partial metamorphosis in dolphin.
Sorry…" he added when he saw the grimace his teammate did at the mention of the mammal.

"You have heard about my grades in transfiguration…" Vlad sighed.

"Everyone in Hogwarts has," the Hufflepuff laughed.
"McGo is endlessly lamenting about your total inability to do any transformation."

"She never tried to help me, and I gave up after the second year.
In any case, I will stop Transfiguration as soon as I can, so it's not a problem."

Cedric heard the little pinch of resentment at the Head of Gryffindor, but did not say anything.
There probably was something else under this conflict, and he was not sure he wanted to know.

"Did you ask for help for the second task?" he eventually asked.

Vladmir laughed at the mention of help.
He remembered a particular detail that he had forgotten to tells his friends when they came back from the seat of the _Underground_, right after they were inducted into the British Court.

He had just finished swimming with his friends when Fred and George had proposed they went to the Leaky Cauldron.
Something about talking businness with Madam Rosmerta, the owner of the pub.

He had crossed paths with Ludo Bagman, busy with hostile Goblins.
The wizard had tried to use him to avoid the magical creatures while offering him some help.
Except thet Vladmir had insisted he greeted the three Goblins who were visibly annoyed at Bagman.

> "§Do not listen to him, Goblin Friend.
> This human is greedy and deceitful.
> His price will be too high for what he offers,§" one of the Goblins had said while Ludo insisted.
>
> "§Thank you for the warning, Forge Master,§" Vladmir said in the same language, making the judge of the Tri-Wizard Tournament very uneasy.
> "§Is He causing problems in the Underworld?§"
>
> The underworld was the true name of the Goblin country, and not Gringott's as many wizards and witches thought.
>
> "§Not yet,§" the oldest of the goblins replied.
> "§Lord Folblang is watching his accounts carefully.
> The human owes us gold…
> Much gold after his bets during the Quidditch World Cup.§"

"I refused Bagman's gracious help," Vlad answered evetually.

"You… what?"

Vladmir, refusing free help from one of the members of the jury?
Cedric was bewildered.

"This guy is an idiot and it makes him suicidal, I do not work with this kind of people," the Russian sighed, before adding for his astounded friend.
"He owes money to the Goblins and he tried to say that they were looking for him about Crouch.
Who is in prison…"

"An idiot," Cedric confirmed.
"To get back to the tournament, if metamorphosis is impossible t oyou, have you tried the charms?"

"Yeah…
The Bubble-Head charm is possible and I have a couple of Dark Magic spells in reserve, we spent quite a while in the Library with Hermione and Kevin…" Vladmir sighed.
"However, they are risky because some circumstances can cancel them."

"Like an injury?"

It was one of the main rules for temporary charms.
They required some focus, and if it was suddenly broken, the situation could get dire.

"Exactly.
As for the potions, I talked with Severus, but…"

"Vlad!"

The two Hogwarts champions stopped to see Neville fly down the stairs of Gryffindor tower, waving a book above his head.

"Look!"

The two champions stepped aside to leave Neville some room.
He held out his Herbology book and pointed to a particular paragraph.
Gillyweed, the plant shown by his friend, was not appetizing on the illustration, but what was interesting was its properties.
It acted on anatomy and transformed the human respiratory system into gills.
And it made palms appear to easy underwater movement.

"I do not know this plant," Vladmir mumbled.

"It's normal, we only find it in the Mediterranean, African side.
It was quite rare, and very expensive…"

"Do you think Severus has some in his laboratory?" the Gryffindor asked.

"I hope…
It's a bit late to get some through the Court," Cedric whispered as Vladmir simply pinched his lips.
He could use his status to the Russians, but it would take his night and…
No, with the tournament and his royal meeting, he would rather sleep.

A few minuts later, they were in the dungeons, knocking on the door of the Potions professor's private apartments.

"What?" Severus spat out, opening the door sharply.
"Ah, it's you, come in!"

The sudden change of behaviour from Professor Prince was both amusing to see and understandable.
He kept his role as a generally hated professor…
Cedric noticed that this time, his hair was clean, which meant that the Potions Master had finished brewing his mixtures for the night.

"Tea?" Severus asked calmly, pushing a pile of papers before looking at the three wizards in front of him, making a tea service appear.
"What is bringing you here at this time?"

"Do you have Gillyweed?" Vladmir asked calmly, leaning over one crossed out paper by an unknown Hufflepuff, smiling at the comments.

"I have some…
You are only thinking about the second task now?" their professor groaned.

"We had other firsh to fry," Cedric grumbled.
"We have other ideas, but this one is the best.
By the way, thanks Neville."

The Gryffindor smiled in response, looking at the shelves of the Potions Master personal library.

"How did you have this idea?" Severus wondered with interest.

Neville simply showed his book and Severus noded, whispering that it was a good and interesting book.
He then stood up to go to one of the doors in his apartment.

"I suppose you want some for you two?" he asked them from his supply cupboard.

"Yes please.
If you could give each of us three hours worth," Vlad completed, remembering that the treaty said it was effective for only an hour.
