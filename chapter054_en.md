# Chapter 54: A Busy Afterparty

"Horcruxes…" Kevin mumbled.
"Fucking Horcruxes…
I had managed to forget those pieces of shit…"

He stared at the moving ceilingof the room he had shared with Vladmir for three years now.
Appeasing hisses were lulling him, his two small familiar snakes understanding his pain through the link they shared.

This annoying reminder added to Kevin's raging headache.
He already felt like he was about to puke when he moved too fast, he did not need another problem.

However, Kevin could only blame himself.
And the worst part was that he knew it perfectly well, through the alcohol.
After all, he had to celebrate the Hogwarts victory in the first Task!

He had had the bad idea to rise up to a challenge a Hufflepuff had given to Vladmir and Aleksei regarding alcohol.
Needless to say, even the seventh years had to bow down to the resistance of the Russians.
Vlad had sniggered and whispered that one glace of Icevodka had the same dose of alcohol as four glasses of Firewhiskey.
So he had drunk the amber alcohol like it was water, before Aleksei decided to finish the most courageous students with a single glass of the Russian national drink.

On that last one, Kevin, Neville and Hermione had been prudent, as were most Slytherins and Ravenclaws in the party.
However, most Gryffindors and Hufflepuffs had followed the Russians.

The results were not glorious, even for Kevin's friends.
Anthony, Micheal and Terry, other Ravenclaws of his year, all went to the park, stumbling, and still had not come back when the party was over at four in the morning.
Draco and Vlaise had ended up in the restrooms with several Hufflepuff students, under Theodore's and Daphne's mocking gaze.
They had enough material to blackmail them until the end of the year, especially with the number of pictures they had taken…
Cedric had simply put his head on the table, before Cho, his probable future ex gilfriend, had forced him to go back to his dormitory.
The witch had threw a fit of jealously in front of the forty or so students from all Houses when Hermione had given hom a peck on the cheek to celebrate his victory.

It was actually one of the highlights of the party.
Cho, feeling the heat of alcohol, had tried to slap Hermione, accusing her to hoarding her boyfriend.
Needless to say, the barely legal curse Hermione had cast in response had shocked many people.
Cedric had tried to intervene when his future ex had jumped on Hermione to surprise her.
'Tried' was the word…
Hermione had a mean right hook when she meant it…
She had thus quickly broke the Ravenclaw's nose.

"Do you think Cho will have beautiful blue eyes?" Kevin asked, his mouth furred.

Vlad sniffed in amusement next to him.

"Surprisingly, you turn out well with alcohol," Vlad smiled, leaning towards his friend, a vial of potion in his hand.
"Drink this."

Kevin obeyed, and his toughts became clearer and his sickness nearly completely disappeared.

"You'll feel queasy until the rest of the alcohol leaves your system.
I can only advise you to eat and drink water."

Kevin nodded and sat up slightly satisfied to feel that neither the ceiling nor the walls were moving.

"And to answer your question, yes, Cho will have big black eyes.
Hermione broke her nose last night."

"Seriously?" Kevin sniggered.
"You'll excuse me, that part fully left my mind when Draco started to kiss her pationately after she put her fist into the bitch's face."

It happened before the small drinking duel started, and Hermione had ended the night sitting down on Draco's lap to talk with her friends, to every one's amusement.

The party had been wonderful… until Vlad had admitted how many Horcruxes he had discovered in his mind when they got to their rooms, a few minutes earlier…

"Do you have a list?" Kevin enquired.

The Russian held out a piece of parchment to him.
Kevin scratched the tip of the head of one of the three snakes snoozing on his pillow.

Kevin's hiss of surprise had some Parsletongue notes in it, catching the attentions of the ice vipers.

`It's alright, loves, go back to sssleep,` Kevin whispered, not looking up from the list.

Six is not a very powerful number.
No strong arithmetic meaning.
That's only six pieces of soul…
We're missing one."

"Probably," Vlad agreed.

A ball…
